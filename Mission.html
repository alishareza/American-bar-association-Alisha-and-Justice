What Is ABA's Mission?


The American Bar Association's (ABA) mission is to advance the rule of law, promote justice, improve the legal profession, provide resources, etc. They complete this by: seeking out opportunities to resolve problems that jeopardize the rule of law and to guarantee that it is maintained, advancing human rights, justice, and an equitable judicial system, publishing books/magazines about law, promoting causes like the Legal Services Corporation's (LSC) financing. For attorneys, judges, law students, and anybody else interested in matters pertaining to the law, the American Bar Association offers information. ABA tries its best to provide resources including model ethics codes, continuing legal education, and accreditation for law schools.

Why was ABA established?

The ABA was founded to establish the nation's legal and ethical standards. It focuses on setting academic standards for law schools, creating ethical codes for legal professionals, and providing practical resources for those in the legal field. These principles were fundamental to the organization's establishment. .
